Climate coverage volume roughly doubled after 2019, remained elevated through COP26 (2021), then in 2022 was dominated by geopolitical energy framing (Ukraine war: 46.8% of dominant topic category).

## Finding 3 — Speaker Type Distribution: Élite-Dominated, Citizens Marginal

### Mention counts (all 56,613 speaker mentions)

| Type | Mentions | % |
|------|--------:|--:|
| M1 Journalist/anchor | 31,034 | 54.8% |
| M6 Politician/official | 12,797 | 22.6% |
| M5 Stakeholder (NGO/business) | 6,240 | 11.0% |
| M3 Scientist/academic | 4,871 | 8.6% |
| M2 Ordinary citizen | 1,143 | 2.0% |
| M4 Pseudoscientist | 87 | 0.2% |

**M4 near-absent:** Only 5 unique pseudoscientist/climate-denier speakers across 10 years. ČT did not platform climate denial or alternative science at any meaningful scale.

**Scientists outnumbered by politicians 2.6:1.** Climate is primarily covered as a political issue, not a scientific one.

**Citizens (M2) are the least heard voice** — 2.0% of mentions despite being the population most affected.

**Top non-journalist speakers:** Richard Brabec (Czech Minister of Environment, 894 mentions), Marian Jurečka (M6, 425), Michal Žák (scientist M3, 371), Andrej Babiš (M6, 307), Václav Cílek (scientist M3, 178).

## Finding 5 — Stance Distribution: Responsible but Not Transformative

### Non-neutral speaker stances (2,103 segments)

| Stance | Count | % of non-neutral |
|--------|------:|-----------------:|
| S6 Informer | 1,728 | 82.2% |
| S3 Delayer | 148 | 7.0% |
| S4 Techno-optimist | 95 | 4.5% |
| S2 Manipulator | 77 | 3.7% |
| S1 Denier | 48 | 2.3% |
| S5 Market-only | 7 | 0.3% |

57.1% of all classified segments are S0 (neutral/procedural) — climate treated as information item, not emergency.

### Stance by speaker type (% problematic = S1+S2+S3)

| Type | % Problematic | % S6 Informer |
|------|-------------:|--------------:|
| M3 Scientist | 2.8% | 45.8% |
| M2 Citizen | 2.8% | 35.2% |
| M5 Stakeholder | 3.9% | 38.3% |
| **M6 Politician** | **7.6%** | **31.1%** |

**Politicians are the primary carriers of delay and denial.** Of 48 S1 (denier) segments, 43 are M6 politicians. Václav Klaus is the dominant S1 voice.

**No scientist (M3) was ever classified as S1 (denier).**

### False balance (P3.5)
10.2% of articles with non-neutral stances contain both a problematic (S1/S2/S3) and an informer (S6) speaker — a form of false equivalence. Spike in 2017 (18.6%) and 2021 (11.5%).

## Finding 6 — Urgency Without Agency: Crisis Language Decoupled from Action

### Urgency/action keyword ratio (per 100 docs)

| Year | Crisis language | Action calls | Ratio |
|------|---------------:|-------------:|------:|
| 2012 | 40.4 | 10.5 | 0.26 |
| 2017 | 46.5 | 5.9 | 0.13 |
| 2019 | 59.5 | 5.1 | 0.09 |
| 2022 | 104.0 | 11.6 | 0.11 |

**Crisis language has doubled; action calls barely moved.** The ratio of action calls to crisis language has declined from 0.26 (2012) to ~0.10 (2019–2022). ČT communicates emergency but not agency.

**Climate justice: structurally absent.** Never exceeds 1.9 per 100 docs in any year. The framing of climate as a justice or equity issue is missing from the entire decade of coverage.

**EU Green Deal:** Near-zero before 2020 (1–3/100 docs), then 25× growth to 25.4 by 2022 — reactive to EU policy events, not proactive editorial framing.

## Finding 7 — Topic Landscape: Politics Crowding Out Science

### Dominant topic categories (NMF k=20, % of documents)

| Category | Overall | 2012 | 2022 |
|----------|--------:|-----:|-----:|
| Science | 19.7% | 33.3% | 12.7% |
| Mitigation | 18.1% | 15.8% | 9.8% |
| Adaptation | 15.2% | 14.0% | 10.4% |
| Policy (CZ+EU+US+International) | 30.6% | 33.3% | ~20% |
| Geopolitics | 3.5% | 0.0% | **46.8%** |
| Activism | 4.7% | 0.0% | 2.9% |

**Key trends:**
- Science-led coverage fell from 33% (2012) to 13% (2022)
- In 2022, Ukraine war / energy crisis captured **46.8%** of climate documents — geopolitics overwrote the climate frame
- COP26 (2021) drove International topic to 20.6% — ČT responds to agenda, not sets it
- Bark beetle forest crisis and drought emerged as major adaptation topics (T10, T12)
- Nuclear energy appears as significant topic from 2019, rising in 2022 (Czech nuclear debate)

### Collocate findings
- "klimatická krize" collocates with: "premiér", "politici", "vláda", "ekonomiky" — crisis framed as political/economic, not scientific
- "globální oteplování" collocates with "stupně", "udržet" — 1.5°C/2°C target language present
- "klimatická změna" collocates with "počasí" (weather) — climate/weather conflation visible

## Finding 8 — Transformative Journalism Assessment

| Criterion | Score | Verdict |
|-----------|------:|---------|
| De-polarization | **7/10** | MEETS for denial; PARTIALLY for false balance |
| Urgency & solution framing | **3/10** | Crisis without agency; procedural treatment |
| Just transformation | **1.5/10** | Elite-only voices; justice absent; Global South invisible |

**Overall conclusion:** ČT covers climate responsibly but not transformatively. It avoids amplifying denial and pseudoscience — a genuine achievement. But it fails on every dimension of transformative journalism: urgency is communicated without solutions, citizens and the Global South are marginalized, gender parity is absent among experts, and climate justice is invisible.

## Data Quality Notes

1. **12 climate false positives** — documents where `klimatický podmínka` was the only keyword (motorway conditions). Impact: 0.4%.
2. **"Terrorism" corpus is debt enforcement** — `exekuce`/`exekutor` = Czech bailiff proceedings, not terrorism.
3. **COVID corpus has pre-2020 articles** — `korona*` matches "koronace" (coronation) and "koronární" (coronary).
4. **Stance coding**: 57% S0 (neutral) partly reflects transcript structure — procedural exchanges coded as non-climate-stance. LLM SKIP rate: 17.3%.
5. **Topic model**: No lemmatization (UDPipe not installed) — inflected forms are separate terms. Journalist names suppressed via M1 stopword list. Note in methods.
